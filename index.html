What You can do to Help Yourself and the Planet

This image known as the Blue Marble has become the most reproduced photograph in all of human history, and it has changed the way that humanity has thought about our common home. It reminds us that we are all connected and that our actions have an impact on the planet. The climate crisis is now the biggest and most serious challenge that humanity has ever faced.

Let me show you some things you can personally do to contribute to lowering greenhouse gas emissions.

This pie chart represents the distribution of the different kinds of greenhouse gases emitted into the atmosphere. Now all greenhouse gases are not created equal in terms of their effect on global warming. So climate scientist have designated GWP as a measure of the relative warming effect compared to Carbon Dioxide which is assigned a value of 1. What you see on the right is the result of scientific studies that have been done periodically.

Percent of GHG from Industrialized Agriculture

  • 53% of CO2 equivalent emissions of N2O (Nitric Oxide)
  • 44% of CO2 equivalent emissions of CH4 (Methane)
  • 5% of CO2 emissions

From Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations

Now the amount of global warming CO2 equivalency due to industrialized agriculture is very hard to measure and as you can imagine it is a controversial topic. These are some of the most conservative estimates coming from a trusted source. A detailed list about the problems caused by industrialized agriculture as well as a video can be found at the factory farming awareness coalition website(See https://ffacoalition.org). Regardless of the exact numbers, clearly industrialized agriculture plays a major role in the climate crisis and it is something all of us can play a major role in changing.

Most farm-related emissions come in the form of methane (cows burping- and large feces pits) and nitrous oxide (synthetic fertilizers) which as I pointed out in the last slide has a much greater effect on thickening the greenhouse gas layer. But it’s also the transportation and energy used in the process of raising and processing meat and the food they need.

Large deforestation is happening around the world to make room for more corn and soybeans for cattle feed as well as industrialized growing of palm oil, and more land for raising livestock. This decreases the CO2 absorption that trees provide, so it’s really important that we’re not taking down the trees in the Amazon and around the world.

So what can you personally do?

Environmentalist Paul Hawken has gathered scientists around the world to measure and model the most substantive solutions to stop global warming, and he has a book called Drawdown and a website called Drawdown.org (See http:/drawdown,org/) where he goes into detail about the most productive things we can do in order of greatest impact for the least cost. In other words, a cost benefit analysis. The list you see here is a subset of his list that individuals can do.

Growth of Plant-Based Foods Market

Luckily, Americans are already starting to make this change. Over 100 million Americans report eating more plant-based protein. Plant-based foods are one of the fastest growing sectors of the food industry. While there are an increasing number of vegetarians and vegans, this change is largely being driven by part-time vegetarians. Have you tried eating Beyond Meat burgers or Impossible burgers? And there are many other plant based meat alternatives for all kinds of meat as well as dairy.
